THE KING OF THE PLANETS

Jupiter, the largest planet in the solar system, is best known for its colorful storms, the most famous being the Great Red Spot.

Now astronomers have focused on another beautiful feature of the planet, using the ultraviolet capabilities of NASA's Hubble Space Telescope and supported by measurements made by NASA's Juno spacecraft.

The Jovian Infrared Auroral Mapper (JIRAM), captured views of Jupiter's north and south poles in infrared light, revealing hot spots that had never been seen before.
